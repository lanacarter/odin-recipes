Indonesian Chicken Soup

Soto ayam is a vibrant, turmeric-infused Indonesian chicken soup served with various toppings like glass noodles and hard-boiled eggs.

The taste is light and refreshing. A warm bowl of Soto Ayam will be perfect in any kind of occasions.

Ingredients

  • Spice Paste (Bumbu): Shallots, garlic, fresh turmeric (or powder), ginger, and candlenuts (macadamia nuts can substitute)
  • Aromatics: Lemongrass (bruised), kaffir lime leaves, and Indonesian bay leaves (salam leaves).
  • Main & Base: Bone-in chicken (thighs or a whole chicken), water or chicken stock, and oil for sautéing.
  • Garnishes: Glass noodles (vermicelli), shredded cabbage, bean sprouts, hard-boiled eggs, fried shallots, and lime wedges.

Instruction

  1. Prepare the Spice Paste: Blend the shallots, garlic, turmeric, ginger, and candlenuts into a smooth paste.
  2. Sauté: Heat oil in a large pot and sauté the spice paste with lemongrass and lime leaves until fragrant and the oil starts to separate.
  3. Simmer the Broth: Add the chicken and water/stock to the pot. Simmer until the chicken is tender (about 30–45 minutes).
  4. Shred the Chicken: Remove the chicken from the broth. Once cooled, shred the meat and discard the bones. Some traditional recipes suggest quickly frying the boiled chicken before shredding for extra texture.
  5. Finalize the Soup: Strain the broth if you prefer a clearer soup, then season with salt, pepper, and a pinch of sugar.
  6. Assemble: In a bowl, layer the soaked noodles, cabbage, and sprouts. Add the shredded chicken and half an egg. Pour the hot broth over the top.
  7. Garnish: Top with fried shallots, fresh celery or spring onions, and a generous squeeze of lime.
